Our History

Long Live Vinyls is an independent record store located in Seattle, Washington. Long Live Vinyls opened its store in West Seattle in 1988, and later added a cafe/bar, which serves coffee, breakfast, lunch and beer & wine. Long Live Vinyls often hosts live in-store performances by national and local musicians. Our store carries new and used CDs, vinyl records, DVDs/BluRays, new books, magazines and company-branded merchandise.

Kudos

In 2010, Rolling Stone named Long Live Vinyls one of the best record stores in the USA and Time magazine included it in its div "10 Great American Record Shops".
Long Live Vinyls is the exclusive retailer for the Sasquatch! Music Festival, which takes place every Memorial Day Weekend in George, Washington. Each year the Long Live Vinyls Sasquatch! booth hosts signing sessions with many of the festival's top performers. Long Live Vinyls is a member of the Coalition of Independent Record Stores (CIMS).

In Store Events

The larger store provided the extra room the owners needed to build a stage and soon national touring artists began performing there, including Pearl Jam, Lou Reed, Elvis Costello, Kings of Leon, Patti Smith, Franz Ferdinand, Lana Del Rey, and many more.
Easy Street is a strong supporter of Northwest artists, and has hosted in-store performances by Nirvana, Mudhoney, The Shins, Macklemore & Ryan Lewis, to name but a few.
To date, the store has hosted over 500 in-store performances.
